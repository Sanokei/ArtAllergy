Our Philosophy

The Manifesto

We have an allergy to the sanitized, the corporate, and the algorithmically optimized. Modern creative industries run on risk aversion. Ideas get focus-grouped, platform-filtered, and smoothed into nothing. We reject all of that.

Art Allergy was born in the drafty lofts of Brooklyn and the damp brick arches of East London. We publish books, zines, art prints, board games, video games, films, and physical merchandise. Every project we back gets produced, distributed, and sold.

New York

Concrete Fever

by Cassidy Vance

Prose Zine

A fragmented, stream-of-consciousness drift through Brooklyn's evolving industrial pockets. Explores structural decay, sensory overload, and late-night municipal sounds.

London

Corporate Solstice

by The Workshop

Deck Builder

A two-player deck-building game set in a brutalist tower block during the longest night of the year. Players draft executives, trade memos, and negotiate hostile takeovers while racing to control the last functional elevator bank.

New York

Outer Boroughs

by Noa Selassie

Art Prints

A suite of twelve risograph prints documenting the last remaining single-screen cinemas in Queens and the Bronx. Each print pairs the theater's marquee with a fragment of its original concession stand signage, shot on 35mm and scanned from hand-developed negatives.

London

Orbital Night

by August Chen & Sade Okafor

Short Films

Five short films shot entirely along the London Overground's night service corridor. Each piece follows a different passenger through the same 3:12 a.m. route from Highbury & Islington to Clapham Junction, shot on Super 8 with original score by a rotating cast of Peckham-based composers.
